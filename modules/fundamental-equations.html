Fundamental Equations

A reference collection of the most important equations used in fluid mechanics, aerodynamics, CFD, turbulence modeling, RANS and LES simulations.

These equations form the theoretical foundation of modern computational and experimental fluid dynamics.

1. Governing Flow Equations

Continuity Equation

\[ \nabla \cdot \mathbf{u} = 0 \]

Conservation of mass for incompressible flow. The divergence-free velocity field ensures that mass is neither created nor destroyed.

Navier–Stokes Equation

\[ \rho \left( \frac{\partial \mathbf{u}}{\partial t} + \mathbf{u}\cdot\nabla\mathbf{u} \right) = -\nabla p + \mu \nabla^2 \mathbf{u} + \rho \mathbf{f} \]

The fundamental governing equation of fluid motion, representing conservation of momentum.

Material Derivative

\[ \frac{D}{Dt} = \frac{\partial}{\partial t} + u_j \frac{\partial}{\partial x_j} \]

Describes the rate of change experienced by a moving fluid particle.

2. Flow Kinematics

Strain-Rate Tensor

\[ S_{ij} = \frac{1}{2} \left( \frac{\partial u_i}{\partial x_j} + \frac{\partial u_j}{\partial x_i} \right) \]

Measures local fluid deformation rates. Important in turbulence production and viscous stresses.

Rotation Tensor

\[ \Omega_{ij} = \frac{1}{2} \left( \frac{\partial u_i}{\partial x_j} - \frac{\partial u_j}{\partial x_i} \right) \]

Represents rigid-body rotation of fluid elements.

Vorticity

\[ \boldsymbol{\omega} = \nabla \times \mathbf{u} \]

Measures local rotational motion in the flow. Central to turbulence dynamics and vortex identification.

3. Potential Flow

Bernoulli Equation

\[ p + \frac{1}{2}\rho U^2 + \rho g h = \mathrm{constant} \]

Conservation of mechanical energy along a streamline for inviscid flow.

Laplace Equation

\[ \nabla^2 \phi = 0 \]

Governing equation for incompressible irrotational flow.

4. Turbulence Fundamentals

Reynolds Decomposition

\[ u_i = \overline{u_i} + u_i' \]

Decomposition of instantaneous velocity into mean and fluctuating components.

Reynolds Stress Tensor

\[ \tau_{ij} = - \rho \overline{ u_i' u_j' } \]

Represents momentum transport caused by turbulent velocity fluctuations.

Turbulent Kinetic Energy

\[ k = \frac{1}{2} \overline{ u_i' u_i' } \]

Measures the kinetic energy contained within turbulent velocity fluctuations.

5. RANS Equations

RANS Momentum Equation

\[ \rho \left( \frac{\partial \overline{u_i}}{\partial t} + \overline{u_j} \frac{\partial \overline{u_i}}{\partial x_j} \right) = - \frac{\partial \overline{p}}{\partial x_i} + \mu \frac{\partial^2 \overline{u_i}}{\partial x_j^2} - \frac{\partial}{\partial x_j} \left( \rho \overline{u_i' u_j'} \right) \]

Time-averaged Navier–Stokes equations used in steady turbulence modeling.

k-Equation

\[ \frac{Dk}{Dt} = P_k - \varepsilon + \nabla \cdot \left[ \left( \nu + \frac{\nu_t}{\sigma_k} \right) \nabla k \right] \]

Transport equation for turbulent kinetic energy.

ε-Equation

\[ \frac{D\varepsilon}{Dt} = C_{\varepsilon1} \frac{\varepsilon}{k} P_k - C_{\varepsilon2} \frac{\varepsilon^2}{k} + \nabla \cdot \left[ \left( \nu + \frac{\nu_t}{\sigma_\varepsilon} \right) \nabla \varepsilon \right] \]

Transport equation for turbulent dissipation rate.

6. Turbulence Modeling

Boussinesq Assumption

\[ - \rho \overline{ u_i' u_j' } = 2\mu_t S_{ij} - \frac{2}{3}\rho k \delta_{ij} \]

Models Reynolds stresses using an eddy viscosity concept. Foundation of most RANS turbulence models.

Eddy Viscosity

\[ \mu_t = \rho C_\mu \frac{k^2}{\varepsilon} \]

Defines turbulent viscosity in the standard \(k-\varepsilon\) model.

Turbulence Production

\[ P_k = - \overline{ u_i' u_j' } \frac{\partial \overline{u_i}}{\partial x_j} \]

Represents transfer of mean-flow kinetic energy into turbulence.

Turbulence Dissipation

\[ \varepsilon = 2\nu \overline{ S_{ij}' S_{ij}' } \]

Represents viscous destruction of turbulent kinetic energy at the smallest turbulent scales.

7. Large Eddy Simulation (LES)

Filtered Navier–Stokes Equations

\[ \frac{\partial \overline{u_i}}{\partial t} + \overline{u_j} \frac{\partial \overline{u_i}}{\partial x_j} = - \frac{1}{\rho} \frac{\partial \overline{p}}{\partial x_i} + \nu \frac{\partial^2 \overline{u_i}}{\partial x_j^2} - \frac{\partial \tau_{ij}^{SGS}}{\partial x_j} \]

LES directly resolves large turbulent structures while modeling only the smallest unresolved scales.

Subgrid-Scale Stress Tensor

\[ \tau_{ij}^{SGS} = \overline{u_i u_j} - \overline{u_i} \ \overline{u_j} \]

Represents the influence of unresolved turbulent motions.

Smagorinsky Model

\[ \nu_t = (C_s \Delta)^2 |S| \]

Classical LES subgrid-scale turbulence model.
