CFU Calculator

Calculate the Convective Flow Unit (CFU), a characteristic convective timescale commonly used in transient CFD and LES simulations to define initialization and statistical sampling durations.

The CFU represents the time required for the flow to convect over a characteristic length scale.

Typically:

  • ~10 CFUs are used for transient initialization
  • ~30 CFUs are used for statistical sampling

Formulas

\[ CFU = \frac{ L_{cyclist} }{ U_\infty } \]

where:

  • \(L_{cyclist}\): characteristic cyclist length
  • \(U_\infty\): freestream velocity

The number of timesteps per CFU is:

\[ N = \frac{ CFU }{ \Delta t } \]
