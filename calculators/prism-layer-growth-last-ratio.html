Prism Layer Growth — Last Ratio Approach

Estimate the minimum number of prism layers required to satisfy a prescribed prism-layer growth rate using a last-ratio approach. This method enssures a smooth transition between the prism cells in the final near-wall grid layer and the polyhedral/tetrahedral/hexahedral cells in the enar volume grid.

Theory

The last-ratio is defined as the ratio between the height of the cells in the last prism layer and the size of the local surface cell. Therefore the final prism-layer height is estimated as a fraction of the maximum or average surface cells size:

\[ h_n = \Delta x \cdot lr \]

where:

  • \(h_n\): final prism-layer height
  • \(\Delta x\): average or maximum surface cell size
  • \(lr\): last ratio (transition ratio)

The minimum number of prism layers is then:

\[ n = 1 + \frac{ \ln\left(\dfrac{h_n}{h_1}\right) }{ \ln(gr) } \]

where:

  • \(h_1\): first prism-layer height
  • \(gr\): prism-layer growth rate
