Open-Section Blockage Correction

Open-section or open-jet blockage corrections account for jet expansion, nozzle/collector effects, wake blockage, and test-object interference. Mercker-Wiedemann is a component-based automotive open-jet correction method. Maskell-style corrections are commonly used for bluff-body wake blockage. Cooper-style methods are practical engineering corrections for bluff bodies in closed and open wind tunnels.

Generic Correction Framework

\[ \varepsilon = K \frac{A_m}{A_j} \] \[ U_c = U_m (1 + \varepsilon) \] \[ q_c = q_m (1 + \varepsilon)^2 \] \[ D_c = D_m (1 + \varepsilon)^2 \]

Method notes:
Mercker-Wiedemann: component-based open-jet method including jet expansion, collector/nozzle effects, and vehicle-induced interference.

Maskell: wake-blockage correction often used for bluff bodies where separated wake growth dominates the interference.

Cooper: practical bluff-body correction framework for closed and open wind tunnels, often used in automotive/aerodynamic testing.
