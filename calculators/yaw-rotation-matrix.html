Yaw Rotation Matrix

Calculate the 3×3 rotation matrix for a yaw rotation about the vertical \(z\)-axis. This is useful for transforming vectors between bicycle, wind, and laboratory coordinate systems.

← Back to Cycling Specific

Formula

\[ R_z(\psi) = \begin{bmatrix} \cos\psi & -\sin\psi & 0 \\ \sin\psi & \cos\psi & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \]

Rotation Convention

Positive yaw rotation follows the right-hand rule about the positive \(z\)-axis:

  • Positive yaw rotates the coordinate system counter-clockwise when viewed from above.
  • A positive yaw angle rotates the \(x\)-axis toward the \(y\)-axis.

For example:

\[ \psi = +90^\circ \] transforms: \[ \begin{bmatrix} 1\\ 0\\ 0 \end{bmatrix} \rightarrow \begin{bmatrix} 0\\ 1\\ 0 \end{bmatrix} \]

meaning that the positive \(x\)-direction rotates into the positive \(y\)-direction.
